Who we are?

Finca Tropical S.A. de C.V. is a legally established in the registry of mercantile property and state with R.T.N.: 05019003087770, and we're dedicated to the Banana crops.

Started operations on October 1th, 2003 and our first installations were located at Finca San Jose (1991-1998), then moved in 1999 to Bamer bananas until September 2003 to the actual location.

The image, quality and prestige of our company is obtained through the help and effort of our employees, collaborators, suppliers and the community, we assure to our clients the best bananas in the national market.

Our Product

The banana that Finca Tropical S.A. de C.V. exports is a 100% Honduran product, at the same time high quality, a unique taste and different, typically of our land. The farm has highly qualified employees and the company keeps capacitating the employees to the process of operation and product logistics.

We count with 239.10 hectares of banana plantation and produce 11,500 weekly boxes of premium Cavendish bananas. The facilities are designed for the production and export of premium quality bananas. We have two brands for local merchandising and exports: COHFRUTSA & BANADOR

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Where can I buy your bananas?

    We sell the bananas at our packaging facility, come visit us and ask for our special prices for resellers.

  • Do you sell just premium Cavendish bananas or also second-hand?

    We do offer several types of quality bananas based on the customer demands. We can meet their requirements.

  • Do you sell only unripe Cavendish bananas?

    Yes! Unfortunately, for now we don’t ripen the bananas.

  • What banana variety do you offer?

    For now we are only selling Cavendish bananas.

  • What are the product presentations?

    The bananas can be packed either on plastic baskets or carton boxes. Based on the payment terms, they can be owned by the buyer or supplier. If the buyer is interested in another packaging material, we can accommodate as well. We don’t find issues, but solutions!

  • What is the price and payment term?

    The price is based on the current banana market price and the volume. We offer competitive prices due to our near location to the port. We are flexible on the Incoterms payment terms: FOB, DAP, DAT, CIF or CFR. Please contact us through our online chat, email (info@fincatropicalhn.com), mobile call (504 – 3188-0380), or social networks. Thank you!

  • Which are the specifications of your Cavendish bananas?

    You can visualize for free our specifications at our website. Furthermore, you can request a visit to our facility through our online chat, email (info@expofrutahn.com), or mobile call (504 - 3188-0380). Thank you!

  • What’s the banana plantation capacity? How many boxes are available for sale?

    We currently count with 239.10 hectares of banana plantation. Approximately, for 18.6 net kilograms per box, our weekly production capacity for premium Cavendish banana is 11,500 boxes and 5,000 boxes for second hand bananas. For the initial buyers, the product availability will be upon request. Consequently, a weekly delivery plan is established with the buyer based on their volume request and after agreeing on price and payment terms.

Certifications

We are a company that respects the environment and excelletng agriculture practices & development.

GLOBAL GAP

The GLOBALGAP standards is primarily designed to reassure consumers about how the food is produced on the farm by minimizing detrimental environmental impacts of farming operations, reducing the use of chemical imputs and ensuring a responsible approach to worker health and safety as well animal welfare. Without these certification the agriculture products may face some obstacle access to the international market requirements

SAG

The SAG (Secretaría de Agricultura y Ganadería) has the objective of maintaining the competitive national agricultural production in the international economy, while keeping all those needs in the local market. This is done with the integration of a human, social and environmental scheme, which is based on self-management, community participation, gender equality and the appropriate sustainable handling of the natural resources.

FDA

The FDA (Food and Drug Administration) is a federal agency of the United States in charged of protecting and promoting the public health through the regulation and the supervision of the food safety.

SENASA

SENASA (Servicio Nacional de Sanidad Agropecuaria) belongs to the National Agriculture Health Organism in charged of the design, direction, coordination and execution of all the animal health and vegetable safeness, dictating the public and private corresponding standards.
